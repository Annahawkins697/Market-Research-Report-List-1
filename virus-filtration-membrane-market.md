<p><p>The virus filtration membrane market encompasses various membrane types, primarily ultrafiltration and nanofiltration. Ultrafiltration membranes are designed to remove larger viruses and impurities from liquids, utilizing a pore size typically between 1 to 100 nanometers, making them effective for applications in water treatment and biopharmaceuticals. Nanofiltration membranes have smaller pore sizes, ranging from 1 to 10 nanometers, allowing for selective separation of smaller molecules, making them suitable for applications such as softening water and separating specific contaminants. Both types are crucial for ensuring product safety and quality across multiple industries.</p></p>
