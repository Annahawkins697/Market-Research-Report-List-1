<p><p>The biopharmaceutical buffers market is segmented into sterile and non-sterile buffers. Sterile buffers are designed for use in aseptic environments, ensuring microbial contamination is minimized, essential for drug formulation and processing within clinical settings. Non-sterile buffers, on the other hand, serve general laboratory and manufacturing purposes where sterility is not a critical factor. Both types play vital roles in maintaining pH levels, stabilizing drug formulations, and ensuring the efficacy and safety of biopharmaceutical products during development and production.</p></p>
